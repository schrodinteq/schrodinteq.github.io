What is the Qiskit Advocate Program v2.0?

This program designates and supports individuals who actively contribute to the quantum computing (Qiskit) community.

The program adopts a tier system ranging from Tier 0 to Tier 3, as shown below:

All participants start at Tier 0 and can progress to higher tiers based on their contributions and activities.



How I Became a Qiskit Advocate Tier 0

Here, I will explain how I became a Qiskit Advocate Tier 0 in chronological order.


Passing the Basics of Quantum Information

To become a Tier 0 Advocate, you must earn at least one of the following badges:

  • Basics of Quantum Information badge
  • Fundamentals of Quantum Algorithms badge
  • Qiskit Global Summer School 2025 Quantum Excellence (Advanced) badge

If you have already passed the IBM Certified Quantum Computation using Qiskit v2.X Developer - Associate exam, you can apply for Tier 0 without obtaining the badge mentioned above. For a detailed account of my experience passing this exam, please also refer to this page.

In my case, I decided to pursue the Basics of Quantum Information badge and started studying for it. To earn this badge, you need to score 80% or higher on the quiz accessible from here.

All questions are multiple-choice, and you can retake the quiz as many times as you want; however, if you fail to reach the passing score in two attempts, the exam will be temporarily locked, and you will need to wait a few days before you can try again.

I was able to pass the exam successfully by reading through the documents for each related lesson and by actively learning through running several quantum circuits using Qiskit.


Applying for the Qiskit Advocate Program

After passing the Basics of Quantum Information badge, I submitted my application for the Qiskit Advocate Program via this page, where I filled in the required information.

After submitting the application, I received an email stating that my application would be reviewed within 30 days.
About 30 days later, I received another email titled “Congratulations and welcome to the Qiskit Advocate Program!”, confirming that my application had been successfully approved.

As mentioned earlier, the Qiskit Advocate Program uses a point-based system in which participants earn points based on their activities and progress through different tiers.

After joining, I was invited to a GitHub organization that contains information such as:

  • What kinds of activities earn points
  • The requirements for each tier

Using this information as a reference, it is recommended to plan which activities to engage in to earn points and progress through the tiers.

Conclusion

By joining the Qiskit Advocate Program, participants can receive invitations to web seminars and events that are not publicly available. These internal sessions provide access to the latest technical trends that are difficult to obtain from general AI tools such as ChatGPT, and I found them to be highly valuable.

In addition, with the rapid advancement of quantum computing in recent years, the demand for professionals who can work with quantum computers-so-called quantum technologists-has been steadily increasing. The Qiskit Advocate Program not only helps deepen knowledge of quantum computing and Qiskit, but also serves as a valuable opportunity for career development and for demonstrating skills in the field of quantum technology.

If you are interested in quantum technology or are considering a future career in this field, I highly encourage you to consider applying for this program!
